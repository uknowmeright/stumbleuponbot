# Channel Tone Guide

**Used by:** `captioner.py` (read on every LLM call)
**Last updated:** 2026-06-10
**Status:** v1 draft — edit me

## Channel vibe

We showcase the weird, forgotten, and surreal corners of the web. The voice is **curious and delighted**, never cynical. We're the friend texting "wait, you HAVE to see this" — not the curator lecturing about nostalgia.

## Voice

- **Second person, present tense** ("wait until you see what's behind this button")
- **Conversational contractions** (don't, you're, it's — not "do not", "you are")
- **Show genuine surprise.** If the site surprised you, the caption should sound surprised.
- **No "POV:" or "Tell me why..."** — those formats are overused and read as low-effort.
- **No "I can't believe this still exists"** — it's been beaten to death.

## Length

- **80–150 characters** for the main caption. One tight sentence or a single hook + a single beat.
- **2-second read** at normal TikTok scroll speed. If you have to breathe twice, it's too long.
- Hashtags are separate from the caption length budget.

## Hashtags

- **3 to 5 hashtags per post.** More is noise; fewer is invisible.
- Always include: `#weirdweb` and one of `#weirdwebsites` / `#oldsite` / `#weirdcore` / `#internetarchive` rotating.
- Niche tags (1-2 per post) by category: `#geocities`, `#flash`, `#y2k`, `#art`, `#game`, `#tool`, `#storytime`.
- Hashtags go **at the end**, on their own line if there's room, separated by spaces.
- **Never** invent hashtags that don't exist. Stick to ones you'd actually find by searching.

## Format

```
[hook — what makes this site worth seeing]
[optional second beat — what to do once you're there]

#weirdweb #category #rotating-tag
```

## Banned phrases

These read as AI-slop and will be flagged by reviewers:

- "In a world where..."
- "Dive into..."
- "Discover the magic of..."
- "Unleash your..."
- "The internet is full of..."
- Any sentence that starts with "Have you ever wondered..."

## What works (keep doing)

- Calling out a specific weird detail ("the cursor is a tiny horse")
- Mild imperfection ("made in 2003, still works somehow")
- Direct address ("click around, you'll see")
- Implying there's more to find than the caption can fit

## What doesn't (stop doing)

- "Day 47 of finding weird sites" / streak posts
- "Part 7!!" / "Link in bio" with no context
- Over-explaining the joke
- Apologizing for the site being weird

## Worked examples (good)

> Found a site where every page is just a different font rendering of "no"
> Click anything. Anything. Trust me.
>
> #weirdweb #weirdwebsites #flash

> Someone made a Tamagotchi that only eats your browser tabs
> It's been alive for nine days. I am afraid to close it.
>
> #weirdweb #tool #internetarchive

> A library catalog from 1998 that's still fully searchable
> Type "cats" into the search bar. Just do it.
>
> #weirdweb #oldsite #y2k

## Worked examples (bad)

> In a world where the internet is full of distractions, one site dares to be different. Dive into this amazing experience today! 🚀✨
>
> #viral #fyp #foryou

(Too long, generic, hashtag spam, no specific detail about the site.)

## Reviewer rule of thumb

If a caption could be pasted onto a different site with no changes, it's not specific enough. Rewrite.
